The Problem

India's job market forgot about India's workers.

Open Naukri. Open LinkedIn. Open Indeed. Now imagine you are Ramesh — a mason from Kanpur with 12 years of experience, hands that have laid the foundations of hundreds of homes, and a Nokia Android phone with 2G internet. You cannot use a single one of these platforms.

They are in English. They require you to type. They assume you went to college. They assume you have a resume. Ramesh has none of these things. But Ramesh has a skill that the city desperately needs, and a family that desperately needs him to find work.

This is not a story about one person. This is the story of 450 million informal workers in India — masons, electricians, plumbers, domestic helpers, drivers, farm labourers, tailors, painters, welders — who form the backbone of the Indian economy and are completely invisible to its digital job market.

450M Informal workers in India with no access to digital job platforms
40% Commission taken by middlemen just to connect a worker to an employer
0 Major job portals designed for Hindi-speaking, low-literacy workers

The middleman — the local "thekedar" or contractor — exists because there is no alternative. He takes 20–40% of a worker's daily wage simply for making a phone call. A mason earning ₹700/day loses ₹150–280 to someone who did nothing but act as a directory. Over a year, that is ₹40,000–70,000 extracted from a family that cannot afford to lose it.

"A mason with 15 years of experience has built 200 homes. He has zero documentation to prove any of it. No certificate. No credential. No digital footprint. He is invisible."

The Idea

What We Built

A bridge. Not another portal.

When we sat down to think about this problem, we made one decision that changed everything: we would not build for the worker we wished existed. We would build for the worker who actually exists.

The worker who actually exists speaks Hindi. Uses WhatsApp. Has a ₹5,000 Android phone. Has never filled an online form. Cannot read English. But can speak fluently and eloquently about their skill, their experience, and what they need.

So we built KaamSetu — काम सेतु, "the bridge to work" — around a single interaction: speak your skill, get matched to a job.

How It Works

The Technology

AI that actually speaks the worker's language.

Under the hood, KaamSetu uses Claude AI to extract a structured skill profile from natural speech. When Ramesh says "मैं राजमिस्त्री हूँ, 10 साल का अनुभव है", Claude understands — not just the words, but the meaning. It extracts his primary skill, related skills, experience, location, and wage expectation in under three seconds.

This profile is then matched against our job database using a combination of keyword and semantic vector search. Jobs are scored on four dimensions: skill compatibility, location proximity, wage alignment, and employer trust score. The worker sees their top matches ranked by relevance — not by who paid for a sponsored listing.

  • 🎙️Voice-first input — Hindi speech recognition via Web Speech API and Bhashini (Govt. of India API), supporting 12 Indian languages
  • 🧠Claude AI skill extraction — Natural language → structured skill profile in under 3 seconds, multilingual
  • 🔍Semantic job matching — pgvector on Supabase for meaning-based search, not just keyword matching
  • 📲WhatsApp bot — Complete job search and application flow without leaving WhatsApp, zero app install needed
  • 📞USSD fallback (*123#) — For feature phone workers with no internet, via numbered menu
  • 🤖Saathi AI chatbot — A Hindi-speaking AI companion for wage advice, govt schemes, legal rights, and emotional support
Saathi

The Chatbot

Every worker deserves a knowledgeable friend.

The most powerful feature of KaamSetu is not the job matching. It is Saathi (साथी) — our AI chatbot companion, built into the app.

Saathi means companion in Hindi. And that is exactly what it is. A patient, warm, knowledgeable friend who happens to know everything about the job market, government schemes, legal rights, and wage negotiation — and explains all of it in simple, clear Hindi.

Most informal workers don't know that PM Kaushal Vikas Yojana offers free skill training. They don't know their state's minimum wage. They don't know they can file a complaint if an employer doesn't pay. They don't know the e-Shram card gives them ₹2 lakh accidental insurance. Saathi tells them. Step by step. In their language.

"When a worker says 'दो हफ्ते से काम नहीं मिला' — Saathi first listens. Then it helps. Because dignity comes before a job listing."

The Impact

Why It Matters

This is not a hackathon project. This is infrastructure.

We think about KaamSetu the way we think about UPI. UPI didn't compete with existing banks — it gave every Indian a payment identity in their pocket. KaamSetu doesn't compete with Naukri — it gives every Indian worker a skill identity, a job pipeline, and a knowledgeable companion in their pocket.

At just 1% penetration of India's informal workforce, KaamSetu eliminates over ₹200 crore in middleman commissions annually. It reduces days-without-work by an estimated 30%. It puts ₹150–280 more per day into the pockets of families who need every rupee.

But the real impact is not in the numbers. It is in the dignity. It is in Ramesh being able to say — for the first time — "I found this job myself."
